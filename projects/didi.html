Project Brief

DiDi is the main ride-hailing app in China with over 550 million users and tens of millions of drivers. This project was one of the three projects that I worked on during my internship at DiDi.

DiDi Saturday Marketing Campaign

DiDi Saturday is the biggest marketing campaign of the year that drives most of the sales. On every DiDi Saturday, users get an automatic 20%-30% off the express rides.

Target User:

Expats living in China

Project Goal:

Inform users about the discount to increase sales.

My Role:

Conduct user interviews and collect feedback on the design.
Design the campaign logo and key visuals for both in-app exposure (Splash Screen / Pop-up Window / banner) and social media exposure (YouTube Video Ads / Facebook Ads / Website Banner).

Campaign logo design

This is something that will be placed in all the key visuals. Through a couple of brainstorming sessions, we narrowed down the campaign name to DiDi Day, DiDi Saturday, and DiDi Super Day.

Drafting the campaign logo

I was given 1 day to come up with as many drafts as possible of the three potential names.

I chose orange as the main colour as:
  • It is DiDi's brand colour
  • It helps make the logo fun and energetic
  • It helps create the promotion vibe
Ones Chosen By the Team

Surveys

My leader and I contacted 23 target audiences in total asking for their opinions on the campaign name, as well as the logo drafts.



To provide more context, I have also created a few drafts to show where the logo will be placed.

There are three elements that we have to include in the final design:
  • The campaign Logo
  • The discount number and the date
  • A Circle (Required by DiDi's brand guide)
Survey Results


The Shadow version received the most positive feedback. A lot of the interviewees mentioned they like the simplicity of it.


We decided to keep the Shadow version and the Vegas version as they were voted as the top two, and the team seems to prefer the Vegas one more.

Key Visuals Design

After brainstorming with my leader, we decided to try the following themes:
  • Matching the illustration with the season to grab users' attention
  • Big city theme to match the environment where our users are
  • Nature theme to encourage our users to go on an outing
  • Abstract theme for experimentation
  • Car theme to make it more "DiDi"
Key Visuals Drafts

I was also experimenting with more new campaign logos to make them blend in with the visuals more.

Interviews

At this stage of the project, we were still experimenting. We decided to conduct a few design interviews with our target audiences to gain more perspectives on the campaign and gather feedback on the drafts.


A short article was posted on our WeChat Official account to recruit participants with a 200RMB gift card compensation.

We selected 4 participants in total (two males and two females.)


List of questions asked before showing the drafts
  • What kind of feelings do you think the key visuals should deliver?
  • How do you envision the key visuals to look like, and how would it change over time?
  • What’s the key to getting people's attention? (based on scenarios)
  • How would you avoid users getting tired of the season-long promotion?
  • Anything that the design should avoid and why?
Draft Critique

After our discussion, I then showed the participants the drafts and ask them to critique both the idea and the design.


To get honest feedback, I told them I was not the one who created the drafts intentionally when in fact they were all designed by me.

Interview Feedback
What I learned from the user interviews
  • City landscape and seasonal elements are ideas that can be kept and improved on
  • There are too much going on in the current drafts
  • None of the draft is good enough and I'm going to have to start from 0 again

September Campaign - Explore Your City

Depending on when our users open the app, the illustration would have a day and a night version to match.


As Beijing and Shanghai are where more than 50% of the sales generated, users in those two cities would get illustrations of the local attractions. For other cities, there is a generic version.

October Campaign - It's fun out there

In China, people tend to go out and travel in October as everyone gets 8 days off to celebrate the birth of the country.


November Campaign - Treat yourself

November is when the largest shopping day(November 11) happens. It's also the golden fall season and Thanksgiving.

December Campaign - Feel the season

It's Christmas!

Takeaways

I'm thankful of my leader to let me conduct the user interviews. I was able to get straight-forward criticism that I don't tend to hear asking feedback from people that I already know. Those feedbacks helped me take a step back and critique my own work.


The English team has also taught me the importance of logic behind every piece of deisgn. I used to think graphic design is just about making things pretty, but through each feedback my leader gave me, I realized the logic behind every single element matters more when it comes to marketing design.
