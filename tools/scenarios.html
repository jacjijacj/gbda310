Scenarios





User scenarios are stories we create to show how users might act to achieve a goal in a system or environment. We make scenarios to understand users’ motivations, needs, barriers, and more in the context of how they would use a design, and to help ideate, iterate, and usability-test optimal solutions.


First, we have to define the following:

  1. Background – who are your users (including their knowledge base and skillset/s)?
  2. Motivations – what goals do they want to achieve?
  3. Tasks – what must they do to reach those goals?
  4. Context of use – how will they encounter your design?
    • Environment  –  where will they try to use it?
    • Challenges  – when they try to use it, what can get in their way (e.g., signal loss)?

And then we can start creating our user story! We can do so by providing the context of:

  1. Who – details of the persona.
  2. What their goals are.
  3. When they might perform tasks (including obstacles).
  4. Where they might do these (including obstacles).
  5. Why they want to do things, must perform subtasks, etc
